Projects

Ame Box
This past summer, I worked at Brazil’s largest e-commerce company, Americanas, as a special projects assistant. My primary responsibilities included creating a proof-of-concept(poc) of a catalog for ride-share drivers to sell products to their passengers, as well as tweak this catalog in order to sell convenience products at office spaces. By building prototypes and getting feedback on it, it become clear that this catalog needed to address the broader opportunity of improving customers' buying experiences while lowering the sign up friction of merchants. The requirements for this poc where that it processed credit card transactions through Americana's Ame Digital Wallet , offer an interactive and customizable list of goods and services, and registered sales with the company's order management system.
Thinking of allowing different buying experiences, I created the following options:
  • Quick Pay

    • This payment option started with the idea of selling products to people at their desks in office buildings, using a plane food cart to go through narrow spaces. The goal was to offer the most convenient possible service, which entailed having the client merely reach out their arm with the payment QR Code on their phone screen. The merchant operating the scart would register the products the client chose, and scan the QR Code to charge the customer.
  • Self-checkout

    • The first live test we made with this product was to have Americana's employees who purchased ready made meals from a notoriously famous cook close to the office to choose their go-to lucnh boxes from a tablet and scan the generated QR Code with their Ame Wallet in order to pay. This was a big improvement over what was being done before, where those who wished to pay with Ame had to line up, and once it was their turn to pay, the cook would type how much the customer owned(with no inventory control), and then scan the customer's QR Code.
  • Mobile ordering
    • THe employees quickly caught on to the idea, and at some points a line would form for customers to pay on the tablet. The solution was to create a webapp in which they could purchase the meal from their phones wherever they were, a deeplink would then prompt them to pay within the app, and they would only need to pick up their lunch from Mrs. Lilia.
