Projects

Ame Box This past summer, I worked at Brazil’s largest retailer, Lojas Americanas, as a special projects assistant. My primary responsibilities included creating a catalog for ride-share drivers to sell products to their passengers, as well as tweak this catalog in order to sell convenience products at office spaces. These tasks had very limited scope, so I used Design Thinking techniques to identify and state the real problem more clearly and petition for my superiors to allow me to pursue a more worthwhile goal for the company. Once having found the right problem to solve, I used several Agile principles to structure the software development process and deliver working software which integrated with the company’s fiscal, and payment systems in 3 months. I was only able to accomplish this feat because of my communication and leadership skills which allowed to learn and get help from people at multiple departments and even other companies. The resilience and determination for results allowed me to solve complex problems in my projects that arise when a 90 year company makes the decision to go digital. I have a natural self-initiative attitude, through which I personally sold $750 worth of its goods in more than 200 sales over a week in order to test the solution. The company has been using this solution as the foundation for their incursion into the small business payment industry since I left.
